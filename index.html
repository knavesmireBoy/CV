PROFILE

Self-motivated and largely self-taught individual who made a decisive contribution to the success and development of the - part - family printing business. Over 35 years I had to master a range of disciplines in responding to the challenges and opportunities presented to an industry fast pivoting towards an entirely digital workflow.

CAREER TO DATE

NORTH WOLDS PRINTERS LTD.

1979 - 2014

  • 79
  • 86

Litho Press Operator

Operated several Komori Litho presses producing a wide range of printed materials on 2 and 4 colour litho presses. For the majority of this period the firm's four colour work was produced on two-colour presses without the benefit of automated controls, despite these limitations the company established an enviable reputation for excellent colour work delivered at speed. Within a few short years I was in a position to provide tuition to new recruits.

  • 87
  • 93

Repro OperatorPart-time Litho Press Operator

Established the pre-press department quickly developing the skills to convert artwork to press-ready final film resulting in huge productivity gains. Duties included liasing with clients, liasing with repro-houses for scanning and chromalin proofing services, maintaining stock levels and platemaking. As the company’s order book grew the department was able to offer an apprenticeship to a former work experience student, who quickly excelled. He would later establish his own graphics company.

  • 93
  • 99

Pre-Press ManagerMac OperatorGraphic Design

The firm invested in digital kit in late 1993 and within due course both members of the department utilised newly learned software skills to provide design solutions for print clients: Quark, InDesign, Freehand, Illustrator and Photoshop. But the core responsibility was developing and adhering to the stringent pre-flight routines that ensured digital output was flawless. My press experience proved invaluable in this regard. I also championed the adoption of pdf as a robust solution for ensuring file integrity when sending files for output down the iSDN line.

As the department transitioned away from conventional techniques I was responsible for guiding investment in hardware, software and personnel as the print industry underwent profound change due to the remorseless advance of digital technologies.

  • 00
  • 14

Pre-Press DirectorCTP/Digital Press Operator

A succession of image/plate setters culminated in the investment in Agfa's Acento chemistry-free plate setter and their Apogee Pre-Press workflow for driving output for litho, and later, digital work. Mastering this software was paramount for the successful operation of the now state-of-the-art pre-press department.

I continued to provide graphic design services for our regular clients and when more than one enquired about an online presence I realised there was whole new order of technologies to explore and devoted a considerable degree of my own time developing the skill-set required for producing bespoke web pages: HTML, CSS, JS, PHP, MYSQL.

My duties once again included printing when the firm finally invested in a digital press late 2009. We opted to add an online saddle-stitch finishing unit to the Konica Minolta c6501 and also exploited the unique advantage of digital by producing variable data alongside standard print materials.

The firm was sold in July 2014, it was time to move on and my partner's pharmaceutical background determined a succession of moves but, along with the dalmatian, we finally settled in Alderley Edge. The most recent refactor was to a database driven site that I had developed to enable client's to upload files, written in php. I do believe that specific languages are secondary to understanding programming paradigms.
